Encryption uses AES-256-GCM (authenticated encryption) with a key derived from your password via PBKDF2-SHA256 (250,000 iterations) and a random 16-byte salt + 12-byte IV generated per message. Salt and IV are stored alongside the ciphertext in the output, which is safe — they are not secret. Anyone without the correct password cannot decrypt or tamper with the message undetected. If you lose the password, the message cannot be recovered; there is no backdoor.
